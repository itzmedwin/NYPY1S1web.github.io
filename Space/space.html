  • Blurting is an active recall technique that involves quickly reading a section of a textbook or study guide and then closing the book and writing down as much of the information as you can remember. Once completed, you then reopen the textbook or the study guide and compare what you have written to the text to see which areas your reacall was good on, and which areas might need more work.

  • Second, brain relies on an external system for capturing, organising, retrieving, and archiving ideas rather than solely your memory. When you document these ideas and package them in an accessible way, you are able to utilise them without needing as much time nor metal space.

  • The Feynman Technique is a study strategy that involves explaining a topic in your own words as sif you were taching it to someone else who has no background knowledge. It helps simplify complex ideas, identify gaps in understanding, and build a stronger foundation of knowledge.

  • Inversion learning is a learning approach that involves studying a topic by focusing on its opposite or inverse. It can help identify blind spots, biases, or assuptions, and encourage critical thinking and problem-solving by considering alternative perspectives.

  • Please try using gap learning. Studies have shown that if every couple minutes you randomly pause during your intense focus sessions and allow you brain to idle, your rates of learning actually increase. They've found that within those small gaps, there is a replay of the neurons at up to 20x the normal speed.
